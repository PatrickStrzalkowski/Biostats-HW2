#c) A paired twosample t-test can be used to determine if there is a difference between the two groups. A t test is used for comparing the means of EXACTLY two groups. We have a control group (2009) and an experiment group (2010) #d)

#data2009=c(91900,95200,61900,73600,114300,117000,129500,74200,112700,152100,90700,89400,127500,142200)
#data2010=c(82900,86700,46900,41300,91600,100400,128400,55900,101700,139100,79300,76100,114100,139800)
pre=c(data21$X2009.catch..tonnes.)
post=c(data21$X2010.catch..tonnes.)
t.test(post,pre,paired=TRUE)
## 
##  Paired t-test
## 
## data:  post and pre
## t = -6.3808, df = 13, p-value = 2.415e-05
## alternative hypothesis: true difference in means is not equal to 0
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  -17975.138  -8882.005
## sample estimates:
## mean of the differences 
##               -13428.57
qt(0.95,13)
## [1] 1.770933

#e) The critical value is (+/-)1.770933 and the observed is -6.3808. #f) Since -6.3808 is further from 0 than -1.770933, we can reject the null hypothesis and state that the oil spill did have an effect on the total mass of the shrimp caught between 2009 and 2010
